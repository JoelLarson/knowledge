This source is another digital copy of *Getting Things Done* with front matter and endorsements that reinforce the book's positioning as a practical system for regaining control amid overload.

## What It Contributes

- Author and publication context for the GTD method.
- Strong framing around reduced stress, trusted execution, and practical rather than purely philosophical productivity.
- Reinforcement that GTD is designed for knowledge work overload, not just generic task tracking.

## Key Takeaways

- GTD is best framed as a stress-reduction and control system, not merely a task classifier.
- Output should stay practical and operational, matching GTD's bias toward executable decisions.

This source is a digital PDF copy of David Allen's *Getting Things Done*. It captures the core GTD workflow, review discipline, and emphasis on turning ambiguous commitments into explicit next actions.

## What It Contributes

- The full book structure around collection, processing, organizing, reviewing, and doing.
- Clear emphasis on "next action" as the practical unit of execution.
- The idea that stress comes from open loops without defined outcomes or actions.
- A strong argument for maintaining a trusted external system instead of holding commitments in working memory.

## Key Takeaways

- Inbox processing is the act of clarifying undefined "stuff" into explicit states and actions.
- A deterministic rule layer aligns with GTD's insistence on explicit decisions, not vague intention.
- The weekly review is not optional housekeeping; it is a core system-maintenance loop.
- Rewrite suggestions should optimize for concrete, verb-first next actions.

## Notable Structure

The table of contents foregrounds three GTD areas:

- workflow mastery
- practical setup and habits
- the behavioral power of collection, next-action thinking, and outcome focus

This source is a large transcript corpus from the GTD podcast as published through GTDnordic and later Vital Learning. It is a supplementary source layer that expands on GTD practice, common questions, and implementation habits.

## Corpus Shape

- approximately 91 podcast episodes
- transcript files in SRT format
- recurring topics include weekly review, next actions, projects, capture, clarify, horizons of focus, calendar use, and waiting-for practice

## What It Adds

- practitioner-oriented elaboration on GTD concepts already established in the book
- many concrete examples of how people struggle with project definition, waiting-for, reviews, and tool usage
- evidence that GTD is taught through repeated reinforcement of a small set of core practices

## Limits

- transcripts are noisy and repetitive because they come from subtitle files
- this corpus should not outrank the book or official written checklist material when the sources disagree
- the best use of this corpus is triangulation and practical nuance, not canonical definition

## Key Takeaways

- repeated questions suggest next actions, projects, waiting-for, and weekly review deserve especially strong deterministic support
- user friction clusters around maintenance and interpretation, not just capture
- implementation guidance should explain the method in plain operational terms

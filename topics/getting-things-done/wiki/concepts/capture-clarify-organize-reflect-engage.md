The GTD workflow is a staged pipeline: capture inputs, clarify meaning, organize into trusted buckets, reflect through review, and engage with the best current action. It is a control loop, not a one-time sort.

## Stage Meanings

- Capture: collect incoming material without yet deciding what it means.
- Clarify: determine whether it is actionable and what outcome or next action it implies.
- Organize: place the clarified result in the correct bucket.
- Reflect: review the system often enough to trust it.
- Engage: choose what to do based on context, time, energy, and priority.

## Groundloop Mapping

- `capture` loop covers capture plus first-pass clarification.
- `review` loop covers reflect plus system hygiene checks.
- `execution` should remain low priority in v1 because GTD action choice is high-trust and context dependent.

## Design Consequences

- `inbox` is a source state, not a semantic class.
- unchanged inbox items should not be reprocessed continuously between review cycles
- structured review cadence matters as much as classification quality
